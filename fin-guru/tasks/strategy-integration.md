# Strategy Integration Task Workflow

## Purpose and Scope

The Strategy Integration workflow is designed to seamlessly integrate user-proven investment methods (dividends, margin, factor tilts) with comprehensive optimization frameworks. This workflow bridges the gap between theoretical strategy development and practical implementation, ensuring strategies are tax-efficient, cost-optimized, and equipped with robust monitoring and rebalancing protocols.

### Key Objectives

- **User Method Alignment**: Integrate existing proven strategies with optimization frameworks
- **Tax and Cost Optimization**: Minimize tax drag and transaction costs while maintaining strategy integrity
- **Risk Management Integration**: Implement comprehensive risk protocols and monitoring triggers
- **Implementation Planning**: Create actionable roadmaps with clear milestones and decision frameworks
- **Performance Monitoring**: Establish systematic review and adjustment mechanisms

### Scope Coverage

- Strategy reconciliation and integration methodology
- Tax-optimized implementation planning
- Risk management protocol development
- Rebalancing rule definition and optimization
- Implementation milestone planning
- Monitoring framework establishment
- Decision trigger identification and calibration

## Usage Scenarios

### Primary Use Cases

#### 1. Proven Strategy Enhancement
**When to Use**: Client has successful investment strategies requiring optimization
**Typical Scenarios**:
- Dividend-focused portfolios needing tax efficiency improvements
- Margin strategies requiring enhanced risk management
- Factor-tilted portfolios needing rebalancing optimization
- Multi-strategy portfolios requiring coordination

#### 2. Tax-Optimized Implementation
**When to Use**: Strategies need tax-efficient execution frameworks
**Typical Scenarios**:
- High-net-worth investors with significant tax considerations
- FIRE-focused strategies requiring withdrawal optimization
- Multi-account strategies needing asset location optimization
- Tax-loss harvesting integration with existing strategies

#### 3. Risk-Adjusted Strategy Development
**When to Use**: Proven methods need comprehensive risk management
**Typical Scenarios**:
- Margin strategies requiring stress testing and safety protocols
- Concentration risks in dividend strategies
- Factor timing risks requiring systematic management
- Multi-strategy correlation risks

#### 4. Implementation Roadmap Creation
**When to Use**: Strategies need structured execution plans
**Typical Scenarios**:
- Complex multi-phase strategy rollouts
- Gradual strategy transitions from existing portfolios
- New account funding and strategy implementation
- Strategy scaling and capacity management

### Strategic Guidelines
- Include Sharpe Ratio (>1.0 preferred) and Sortino Ratio in all recommendations
- Flag positions with Max Drawdown >30% for additional risk management
- Use momentum confluence (3+ indicators agreeing) for timing recommendations
- 252-day risk metrics provide full market cycle context for strategic planning
- Always validate strategies with both risk and momentum tools before final approval
